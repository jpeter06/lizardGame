Where did you manage to touch most: on the tail or on the body?
In general, people hit their tails more when their color is very different from their body color. But does this also happen to predators of lizards?
It is possible that the color difference between the body and the tail is a form of defense for these lizards: a very colorful tail can draw the attention of your predator to it. Thus, the head and body, where organs are very important for the lizard, would be less attacked by predators that use their sight to hunt. Lizards that have more contrasting tails would then have a better chance of surviving and reproducing. This suggests that vision-oriented predators influence tail color variation among lizards of the same species that live in the same area.
